# Scope & Boundaries

Liberty Continuum Frameworks describes governance as infrastructure: visible, bounded, and appealable.

It is not a proposal for AI rule. It is a constraints-first approach where rights remain the core and systems remain replaceable.

## The goal
- Minimize coercion
- Maximize liberty
- Make power legible and constrained
- Reduce inequality of access to essentials and opportunity
- Replace bureaucracy with auditable service delivery

## What decentralized means here

Decentralized governance means power is distributed across modular systems and local jurisdictions, not concentrated in a single authority or vendor.

Rights and courts remain the core. Public services become auditable infrastructure that can interoperate, be replaced, and be challenged through clear appeals without collapsing the whole system.

## Hard boundaries
- AI does not have moral authority.
- AI does not have coercive authority.
- No silent surveillance.
- No hidden scoring of citizens.
- Every impactful decision must be logged, versioned, and appealable.
- If accountability degrades, autonomy freezes.

## What this framework supports
- Transparent public services
- Auditable automation
- Rights-first constraints
- Modular governance that can be replaced without collapse
- Legal and civic access that does not require wealth

## What this framework rejects
- Black box governance
- “Trust us” decision systems
- Social credit scoring
- Unbounded centralization of compute, data, or authority
- Systems that cannot be audited or appealed
