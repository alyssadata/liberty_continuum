# DEFINITION — Auditability

## Definition
Auditability is the property that a system’s impactful decisions can be inspected after the fact with enough traceability to verify inputs, constraints, outputs, and accountability.

## Required properties
A decision is audit-ready when it includes:
- A decision record
- The system identity and version
- The inputs used and their sources
- The constraint or rule references applied
- The output and its effect
- The appeal path

## Not this
Auditability is not:
- “Trust us”
- Hidden logic
- Unlogged automation
- Non-reproducible outcomes
