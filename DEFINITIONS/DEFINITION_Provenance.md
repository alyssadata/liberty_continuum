# DEFINITION — Provenance

## Definition
Provenance is the traceable origin of decisions, outputs, and changes: who authorized them, what system produced them, what inputs were used, and what versioned constraints applied.

## Required properties
- System identity and version
- Change history for rules and models
- Input source listing
- Authorization trail
- Verifiable logs resistant to tampering

## Why it matters
Without provenance, power becomes deniable.
With provenance, power becomes accountable.
